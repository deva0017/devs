3Ô∏è‚É£ Operation Process

  1. Sunlight falls on the solar panels, producing DC electricity.

  3. The pump lifts water from a source (well, river, or tank) to storage tanks or directly to fields.
  4. Water is distributed through drip irrigation or sprinklers to the crops.
  5. If batteries are included, excess energy is stored for use during low sunlight or peak irrigation demand.

üåû Source of Energy for Solar-Powered Irrigation Pumping

The primary source of energy for a solar-powered irrigation system is solar radiation from the Sun. Solar energy is abundant, renewable, and freely available, making it ideal for off-grid agricultural applications.

Solar Radiation (Sunlight)

Sunlight consists of photons carrying energy. When these photons strike solar photovoltaic (PV) panels, they excite electrons in the semiconductor material (usually silicon), generating direct current (DC) electricity.

Conversion Process

  • Solar PV panels convert sunlight ‚Üí electrical energy.
  • The electricity generated can either be used immediately to run the pump or stored in batteries for later use (e.g., at night or during cloudy weather).

Advantages of Solar Energy for Irrigation

  • Renewable: The Sun is a limitless energy source.
  • Predictable: Solar availability can be estimated based on daily and seasonal sunlight patterns.
  • Off-Grid Friendly: No need for diesel generators or connection to the electrical grid, ideal for remote farmlands.
  • Sustainable: Reduces carbon emissions and operational costs compared to conventional fuel-powered pumps.

Application in Agriculture

  • Powering water pumps to lift water from wells, rivers, or storage tanks.
  • Enabling efficient irrigation systems such as drip or sprinkler systems.
  • Supporting crop productivity in regions without reliable electricity supply.
