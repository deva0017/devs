Micro Tidal Stream Power Plant

Architecture for Sustainable Coastal Village Electrification

Approach: Tidal Stream Generation (TSG)

The chosen architecture is the **Tidal Stream Generator (TSG) System**. This approach harnesses the kinetic energy of moving water, which is more scalable and environmentally friendly for a small village than a large tidal barrage. It focuses on a small array of **Micro-Hydrokinetic Turbines ($\mu$-HKT)** to meet local, decentralized energy needs.

1. Energy Capture: Tidal Turbines

Tidal Turbines ($\mu$-HKT)

These are small, propeller-type turbines (1 kW to 100 kW range) designed for **Bidirectional Operation** to generate power during both the **flood tide** (incoming) and the **ebb tide** (outgoing). They must be of **Robust Design** to resist corrosion and strong currents.

Mounting & Protection

Turbines are secured using a **Support Structure** like a **Fixed/Gravity Base** on the seabed or a **Floating Platform** for easier maintenance. **Fish Screens** or exclusion systems are mandatory to protect marine life and prevent debris damage.

2. Power Conversion and Conditioning

Generator

Directly coupled to the turbine shaft, it converts rotational mechanical energy into raw, variable **Alternating Current (AC)** electricity.

Rectifier & Inverter (PCS)

The **Rectifier** converts variable AC to stable **DC power**. The **Inverter** then converts the DC back into high-quality **AC power** that perfectly matches the required village grid standards (voltage and frequency).

Control System

A microprocessor system that constantly optimizes turbine speed for maximum power (MPPT) and precisely manages the charging and discharging of the Battery Energy Storage System (BESS).

3. Storage and Distribution (Micro-Grid Integration)

Battery Energy Storage System (BESS)

  • **Purpose:** Stores excess energy to ensure a **continuous supply** during **slack tide** (zero current) periods.
  • **Grid Stabilization:** Provides voltage regulation and acts as a buffer against sudden changes in load, ensuring a stable **Micro-Grid**.

Grid Connection & Distribution

  • **Submarine Cable:** Transmits power from the offshore turbines to the onshore Control House.
  • **Step-up Transformer (Optional):** Used for efficient transmission if the distance is long.
  • **Village Distribution:** The BESS and Inverter connect directly to the local low-voltage network, serving local **Village Loads**.
